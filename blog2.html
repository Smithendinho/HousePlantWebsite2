5 House Plants That Help Keep Mold at Bay

Mold can be found in just about any damp, cool place – so it’s no surprise that it can take over your house if you aren’t careful. That’s why it’s important to keep plenty of plants inside your home to keep the air healthy and mold-free. These five houseplants are the best at taking care of mold while they make your home look gorgeous

Aloe vera

Aloe vera is a great house plant that can help keep mold away. It's also one of the easiest plants to care for because it doesn't need much water or light. Aloe vera also works well in any room, as long as it gets plenty of sunlight.

Boston fern

Boston ferns are a great option because they tolerate low light and low humidity. They also need to be watered less often than other plants, which is ideal for people who find themselves forgetting to water their plants often. Boston ferns also do not require much space and can grow up to three feet in height.

Snake plant

The snake plant is a fantastic option for those who want to cut down on the amount of time they spend caring for their plants. It's also great for those who have a lot of indoor plants because it doesn't need as much light or watering as other houseplants do. The green color of this plant will also help brighten up any space.

Golden Pothos

Golden pothos are a great houseplant to help keep mold away. They are also relatively easy to grow and maintain, making them a perfect choice for the busy homeowner. Golden pothos can thrive in a variety of light conditions and locations (bright indirect sunlight or shade), so they will work well in homes with less than ideal windows. Pruning is required about once every three months to maintain their shape and prevent them from getting too leggy.

Peace Lily

Peace lilies are one of the best plants you can have in your home if you're battling mold. Peace lilies naturally clean the air and they also produce airborne substances that inhibit mold growth. Plus, they're beautiful!
