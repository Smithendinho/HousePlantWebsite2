House Plants to Keep in Low Light

If you live in a house that gets little natural light, plants can be the difference between living in a garbage-strewn room and having a place to relax. We've put together this list of low-light houseplants that will help you create an oasis of greenery in your home:

Venus fly trap

Venus fly traps are carnivorous plants that can be found in the rainforest, but can also be grown indoors. The Venus flytrap traps insects by having snap-like jaws that close when an insect lands on its surface. If the trap snaps shut, it will trap and digest any insect that steps on it.

If you don't have a terrarium or other type of container with high humidity levels and low light, this plant can still thrive as long as there is enough water available at all times (about once every two weeks). Just make sure not to overwater them!

Spider Plant

Spider plants are easy to care for and require little water, making them ideal for low-light environments. They can grow in most conditions and naturally repel slugs, snails and other small pests. In fact, spider plants are so attractive that they have become a popular indoor plant choice for restaurants!

Spider plants are also easy to propagate: simply take cuttings from a healthy leaf or stem and place them in soil until they begin to root themselves. Once they're rooted, transfer the baby plants into their own pots by pushing them gently into the soil using a finger or spoon (do not use your hands).

Snake Plant

Snake plants are one of the most popular house plants around. They’re easy to grow, require little maintenance and look great in any home. Plus, they help purify the air by removing formaldehyde and other pollutants from our indoor spaces. The best part about snake plant care? You may want to add a few more of these green beauties into your life!

Peace Lily

Peace lily (Spathiphyllum) is a tropical plant that can be found in the wild, but it is also popular as an indoor houseplant. This plant has beautiful flowers and leaves that look like they’re made of feathers. The peace lily is native to South America, where it grows on trees. It was originally thought that the peace lily only had one leaf, but scientists have recently discovered another leaf in some specimens! The peace lily loves full sun exposure and moist soil conditions with plenty of water. If your peace lily doesn't get enough light or drainage, its leaves might turn yellowish or brown before dying completely off completely.

You can grow this plant by purchasing seeds from any reputable gardening store or online retailer; just make sure you buy them from somewhere reputable so you know what kind of seeds were used for planting purposes! Once your seedlings have sprouted up nicely (usually within 5 days), transplant them into pots filled with potting soil mixed with sand or perlite if needed.

Dwarf Christmas Tree

A dwarf Christmas tree is a small evergreen plant that can be grown in the ground or in a pot. The leaves are small, glossy green and have red berries on them. They're easy to maintain and they make great houseplants because they grow well under low light conditions indoors as well as outside!

If you've ever had to put your house plants in a dark room or under fluorescent lights, you know how important it is to have the right kind of house plants. The best ones are low-light plants that don't require much light in order to thrive. That's why we've rounded up some of our favorite low-light houseplants here at Tropify.
